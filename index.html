Hi, I am a User Experience Designer and Engineer with a background in human-computer interaction research. I am currently at Lautsprecher Teufel, where I work on consumer audio products such as wifi speakers, gaming headphones and soundbars. Some of my daily activities are conducting user research, prototyping of physical and digital features, collaborating with engineering and product teams, and creating UX design specifications.

    Previously, I completed my PhD at Department of Computer Science at University of Copenhagen. I researched within the field of human-computer interaction with a focus on virtual and augmented reality. Among other things, I researched portal-based interaction techniques, navigation of spatial recordings, and learning of motor skills by autocorrecting users virtual movement. You can find the list of my publications at the end of the page.

      In the past, I also worked at relyr where I developed and deployed industrial IoT solutions. For example, I worked on a IoT sensor kit and dashboard for monitoring trains and clocks at DB train stations.

      In my spare time I enjoy reading about latest technologies, creating mixed-reality experiences, and tinkering with arduino.
